The Problem: Why Blood Pressure and Belly Fat Stay Stubborn

If you have struggled with high blood pressure or stubborn belly fat that refuses to go away despite diet and exercise, you are not alone. According to the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention, nearly half of all American adults have hypertension, and many also carry excess weight around the midsection that increases cardiovascular risk.

One factor that often gets overlooked is homocysteine, an amino acid that the body produces during normal metabolism. When homocysteine levels rise too high, research has linked it to elevated blood pressure, blood vessel damage, and difficulty losing weight, particularly in the abdominal area. Standard blood pressure medications often address symptoms without targeting this underlying biochemical imbalance.

What is homocysteine and why does it matter? Homocysteine is an amino acid that, when elevated, has been associated with cardiovascular issues and metabolic problems. Reducing homocysteine through diet and supplementation may support healthier blood pressure and metabolism.
